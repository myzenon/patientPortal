Arine Inc. Access to Another Adult's Online Medical Record 

Requirements and Procedures


Adults who help manage the medical care of another adult, can access the online medical record of the patient, if the patient authorizes.
 
Requirements for online access to a patient’s medical record:
  • Individual requesting access must have signed consent from the patient
  • Authorization Form must be completed and signed
  • Each individual requesting access must have their own Arine account or an account will be established by Arine
 
I understand that:
  • I must have an Arine account or an account will be established for me
  • I must log in to Arine with my own Username and Password
  • I must enter a patient’s unique Health Plan Identification Number to access the patient’s Arine online record
  • Arine is not to be used in an emergency or as a replacement for a physician consultation
 
Access to a patient’s record is revoked when the patient or physician submits a request or revokes access online. Arine reserves the right to revoke online access to medical information at any time. Access to a patient’s record is also automatically terminated upon the patient’s death. 

Communications on behalf of the patient will be tied to the patient’s Arine record and any responses will be delivered to the patient’s Arine record, which you have access to.
 
Arine email alerts related to the patient will be sent to the email address entered in the Arine Caregiver Registration. 

When signed into another person’s Arine record, you will be prompted to confirm that you are assisting the patient that has consented you to view their record. This will serve as an indication that you are in the proper record. 

If you have an Arine account, and have obtained consent from the patient you are assisting, you will receive immediate access to the patient’s record.
